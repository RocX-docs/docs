# What DeFi missed

DeFi proved that capital can move on-chain.

It created open markets, permissionless access, and new ways to use liquidity.

But it left one question unresolved.

Why do so many users leave as soon as the rewards end?

> Liquidity can be attracted.
>
> Participation has to be earned.

---

## The real problem

The problem is not that DeFi failed to bring in capital.

The problem is that most DeFi systems do not know how to keep participation alive.

When incentives are high, users arrive. When incentives fade, activity disappears with them.

<Info>
DeFi became good at attracting liquidity. It has been much less effective at building lasting participation.
</Info>

## What gets lost

Most protocols measure what is easy to count.

Deposits. Volume. TVL. Transactions.

Those numbers matter, but they do not explain whether a user actually belongs to the ecosystem.

They do not show who stayed, who contributed, who returned, or who earned trust over time.

## Traditional DeFi and RocX

| Traditional DeFi | RocX |
| --- | --- |
| Measures capital | Measures participation |
| Optimizes for TVL | Optimizes for retention |
| Uses short-term incentives | Builds long-term engagement |
| Starts from collateral | Starts from activity |
| Records transactions | Builds reputation history |

<Tip>
RocX does not argue that liquidity is unimportant. It argues that liquidity alone is not enough to create durable finance.
</Tip>

## The RocX approach

RocX is not designed around making TVL larger.

It is designed to make participation visible, memorable, and useful.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Record" icon="clipboard-list">
    Meaningful activity becomes part of the user's on-chain history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retain" icon="repeat">
    Long-term participation becomes a signal the ecosystem can recognize.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reward" icon="badge-check">
    Trust and consistency can become access to better financial opportunities.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Good finance should not only gather liquidity.

It should give people a reason to stay.
</Note>
