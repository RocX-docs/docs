Web3 Activity Verification organizes activity from multiple ecosystems into signals RocX can interpret.

The goal is not to collect every possible action. The goal is to identify activity that is meaningful enough to become part of a user's financial context.

<Info>
Verification turns scattered Web3 activity into usable activity signals.
</Info>

---

## Activity starts outside one protocol.

A Web3 user may participate across many places.

They may use DeFi protocols, complete partner campaigns, test new networks, join governance, verify NFT ownership, contribute to communities, or interact with emerging AI, RWA, ZK, and BTCFi ecosystems.

Most of these actions are fragmented. They live across different chains, apps, communities, and campaign systems.

RocX creates a way to bring those activities into one interpretable structure.

---

## Verification adds context.

A raw transaction does not explain everything.

RocX needs to understand what happened, where it happened, how reliable the signal is, and whether the activity should matter inside the RocX ecosystem.

That context helps separate meaningful participation from low-quality repetition.

---

## Verified activity can move forward.

Once activity is verified, it can become part of the RocX value path.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Activity is detected">
    RocX identifies activity from supported ecosystems, campaigns, or product interactions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activity is interpreted">
    The activity is reviewed against category, relevance, quality, and reliability criteria.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activity becomes usable">
    Verified activity can flow into Active Energy, reputation, and benefit criteria.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Verification protects the economy.

Activity verification is also a quality control system.

Without verification, benefits can be captured by repetitive or low-value behavior. With verification, RocX can direct benefits toward activity that strengthens the ecosystem.

<Info>
Verified activity is the foundation for Active Energy, reputation, and activity-based benefits.
</Info>
