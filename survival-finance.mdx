# A new financial layer

Finance has always been good at measuring capital.

It can count deposits, assets, collateral, and transactions.

But it rarely remembers the human side of finance: time, activity, trust, and contribution.

Survival Finance exists to add that missing layer.

> Capital moves.
>
> Participation compounds.
>
> Trust endures.

---

## What Survival Finance adds

Survival Finance is a financial layer where participation becomes part of the user's financial context.

It does not treat every user as a new wallet with no history. It recognizes that people build value by staying active, returning over time, and contributing to the ecosystem around them.

<Info>
Survival Finance turns long-term participation into signals that can support reputation, identity, and financial access.
</Info>

## From capital data to participation data

Traditional finance uses credit history.

DeFi uses capital, liquidity, and transaction data.

Survival Finance adds another signal: how a user participates over time.

| Traditional Finance | DeFi | Survival Finance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Credit history | Capital and liquidity | Participation history |
| Financial records | TVL and transactions | Reputation |
| Assets and income | Collateral and volume | Activity |
| Identity documents | Wallet activity | Proof of Activity |

## The RocX perspective

RocX does not see capital as the only meaningful asset in finance.

Time matters. Activity matters. Trust matters. Consistent participation matters.

These signals can become financial assets when they are recorded, recognized, and connected to opportunity.

<Tip>
Survival Finance values `how long and how meaningfully you participated`, not only `how much you hold`.
</Tip>

<Note>
The goal is not to make liquidity compete harder.

The goal is to make participation worth staying for.
</Note>
